Module 01

🎣 What Is Phishing?

Phishing is a type of social engineering attack where cyber criminals impersonate trusted entities to steal sensitive information like passwords, credit card numbers, or company data.

  • 📧
    Email Phishing

    The most common type. Attackers send mass fake emails pretending to be banks, PayPal, Amazon or IT departments.

  • 🎯
    Spear Phishing

    Targeted attacks on a specific person or company using personalised information gathered from LinkedIn, social media, or data breaches.

  • 📱
    Smishing (SMS Phishing)

    Fake text messages claiming you won a prize, your package is stuck, or your account is locked.

  • 📞
    Vishing (Voice Phishing)

    Fake phone calls from "tech support", "IRS", or "your bank" pressuring you to reveal info or install software.

  • 🐋
    Whaling

    Phishing targeted at C-level executives (CEOs, CFOs) to authorise large wire transfers or leak sensitive corporate data.

🚩 How To Spot a Phishing Email

Click each email below to reveal whether it is a phishing attempt and why.

From: security-alert@paypa1.com Today 09:14 ⚠️ URGENT: Your PayPal account has been limited!
Dear Valued Customer,

We have noticed suspicious activity on your account. You must verify your information within 24 hours or your account will be permanently suspended.

Click here to verify now →
🚨 PHISHING — Do NOT click!
  • Sender domain is paypa1.com (numeral "1" instead of letter "l")
  • Creates artificial urgency ("24 hours", "permanently suspended")
  • Vague greeting — real PayPal uses your full name
  • Link does not point to paypal.com
From: noreply@github.com Yesterday 14:32 Your pull request was merged 🎉
Hi alex_dev,

Your pull request #142 — Fix navbar responsive bug was merged into main by johndoe.

View pull request →
✅ SAFE — Legitimate email.
  • Sender is from the real github.com domain
  • Uses your actual username — not a generic greeting
  • No urgency, no threats, no requests for sensitive info
  • Content matches a real action you might have taken
From: it-support@company-helpdesk.net Monday 08:01 ACTION REQUIRED: Reset your company password
Hello Employee,

Our systems detected your password will expire TODAY. Click the link below to reset immediately and avoid losing access to all company systems.

https://company-helpdesk.net/reset-password?token=xA8k2
🚨 PHISHING — Classic IT impersonation!
  • Company IT would use your company's domain, not a generic "company-helpdesk.net"
  • Generic greeting "Hello Employee" — IT knows your name
  • Extreme urgency ("TODAY") to prevent careful thought
  • Hover the link — the domain does not match your employer's
Module 03

🧠 Social Engineering Tactics

Attackers exploit human psychology — not just technology. Here are the six core manipulation tactics:

  • ⏰
    Urgency & Scarcity

    "Act NOW or lose access forever!" Forces fast decisions without careful thought.

  • 😨
    Fear & Threats

    "Your account will be arrested!" Panic bypasses rational thinking.

  • 🏛️
    Authority

    Impersonating the CEO, HR, IRS, or Microsoft to demand compliance.

  • 🎁
    Rewards & Greed

    "You've won an iPhone! Click to claim." Too-good-to-be-true offers are almost always false.

  • 🤝
    Familiarity

    Attackers study LinkedIn/social media to mention real names and events, building false trust.

  • 🔄
    Reciprocity

    Offering a gift or "helping" you first, so you feel obliged to share information in return.

💡 The Golden Rule
If a message creates strong emotion — panic, excitement, curiosity — pause before you click. That emotional spike is exactly what attackers want.
Module 04

🛡️ How To Stay Safe

🔍

Inspect the Sender

Check the full email address, not just the display name. Hover over links before clicking.

🔐

Use Multi-Factor Auth

Even if a password is stolen, MFA blocks attackers. Enable it everywhere — email, banking, work accounts.

🌐

Go Direct

Instead of clicking email links, type the official website address in your browser bar yourself.

📞

Verify Out-of-Band

Received an unusual request from your "boss"? Call them on a number you already know — not the one in the email.

🔄

Keep Software Updated

Phishing sites often exploit browser or OS vulnerabilities. Updates patch these holes.

🚨

Report Suspicious Emails

Use your email client's "Report Phishing" button. This protects your whole organisation.

💾

Regular Backups

If ransomware is delivered via phishing, offline backups mean you can recover without paying.

🧠

Security Awareness Training

Stay current. Attackers adapt their tactics constantly — ongoing education is essential.
